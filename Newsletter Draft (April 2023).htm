The Lean Lens: Data Analytics

 

Data Analytics/Science, like other domain of sciences, stems from questions which are developed in human thoughts in daily life. The four questions which drive the Data Science are the following:

  What happened?

  Why did this happen?

  What might happen in the future?

  What should we do next?

 

     Descriptive Analytics

It allows to pull trends from raw data and succinctly describe what happened or is currently happening.

     Diagnostic Analytics

It compares coexisting trends, uncovering correlations between variables, and determining causal relationship.

     Predictive Analytics

With historical data in tandem with industry trends, one can make informed predictions about what the future could hold for the business.

     Prescriptive Analytics

Provides recommendations for possible outcomes.

 

 

Data Science aims to answer above questions by considering blocks of data. Application of the Data Science/Analytics within an organisation is based on which of the above questions is the requirement for the business for a specific problem. Each of the above questions has its own specific group of tools in Data Science to be addressed, with a group-label for each:

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 


The four types of data analysis should be used in tandem, to create a full picture of the story data tells. To understand business current situation, use descriptive analytics. To figure out how your business got there, leverage diagnostic analytics. Predictive analytics is useful for determining the trajectory of a situation. Finally, prescriptive analytics can help business plan actionable strategies.

 

 

 

RCA: Kilkenny Food Company

 

 

 

This months Spotlight RCA is from Kilkenny Food Company, which effectively solved an issue whereby there was a batch of soup rejected due to damage to plastic lids occurring.

The unknown cause was solved by collaboration between the Quality (Trish Ryan & Breda OShea), Maintenance (Ben Hayes), and Production (James Muldowney, Liam Scannell & Gabriel Talpes) teams on site, using the 5W + 1H & Fishbone diagram to investigate potential areas that may have contributed to the problem. The corrective and preventative actions carried out have successfully closed the problem.
